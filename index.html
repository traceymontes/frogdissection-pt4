Materials

  1. Preserved frog specimen
  2. Dissection tray
  3. Dissection kit (scissors, forceps, scalpel)
  4. Gloves and safety goggles
  5. Paper towels
  6. Plastic bags for waste disposal
  7. Water for rinsing

Overview

Frog dissection is a common activity in biology labs, providing students with hands-on experience in anatomy. Here's a basic set of steps for frog dissection. Always follow safety guidelines and use appropriate tools and equipment.

Dissection Steps

  1. Place the frog on its back.
  2. Cut through the abdominal wall.
  3. Examine the organs carefully.
  4. Make Incisions
  5. Expose Abdominal Cavity
  6. Remove Organs
  7. Examine the Circulatory System
  8. Respiratory System
  9. Urogenital System
  10. Nervous System
  11. Clean-Up
